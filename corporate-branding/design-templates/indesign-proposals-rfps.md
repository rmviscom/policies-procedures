# InDesign: Proposals / RFPs

#### Standard design templates for proposals can be found in Egnyte:

```
/Shared/Marketing Department/Sales/Templates/Proposal Templates
```

{% embed url="https://robinsmorton.egnyte.com/fl/791TRt5m3p" %}

### Which file to use

<details>

<summary>If your proposal will be printed and bound</summary>

[**Proposal Template\_Print Margins.indt** ](https://robinsmorton.egnyte.com/navigate/file/a6435b09-94de-42ce-bca0-aa8fde8726c9)\
Default Proposal template with .75in left margin for binding/hole punch

![](<../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-01 at 9.39.09 AM.png>)

</details>

<details>

<summary>If your proposal will be delivered digitally</summary>

[**Proposal Template\_DigitalMargins.indt** ](https://robinsmorton.egnyte.com/navigate/file/cad43474-d587-486f-8533-75cdb4e0ea3b)\
Proposal template with .5in margins all around, for when binding isn’t an issue

![](<../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-01 at 9.39.22 AM.png>)

</details>

<details>

<summary>If you want a horizontal layout</summary>

[**Proposal Template\_Digital Horizontal.indt** ](https://robinsmorton.egnyte.com/navigate/file/cb22cfff-2b77-41db-a4d0-b146204a9b6f)\
Proposal template with section headers with room for content, rather than whole page section tabs

![](<../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-01 at 9.45.55 AM.png>)

</details>
