# Board Report Process

## Overview

The board report is produced **four times per year** or once a quarter.

Our team is responsible for the following items for the board report:

* **In-progress project photos:** Robin Savage selects specific projects for the project update section. [Amanda Bradley Witteck](mailto:abradley@robinsmorton.com) coordinates the project updates section of the Board Report. Before each board meeting, she will request aerial photos of the selected projects from the marketing team.&#x20;
  * Marketing team members are expected to communicate with their designated project teams to gather the most recent aerial photos. These photos are included as a part of each project update and uploaded to OpenAsset.
* **Written project update:** The project operations manager or project executive provides a written summary of the projectâ€™s status.&#x20;
  * Our communications team reviews the write-up to ensure consistent brand language use. Each marketing team member may also be asked to review the project updates as an additional secondary set of eyes, or to help confirm project schedule dates or other key components.

Once all photos have been gathered and all written content is complete, Amanda Bradley Witteck submits this portion of the report to [Lisa Cowan](mailto:lcowan@robinsmorton.com).

* **Quarterly Sales Update:** Farrah Mote and Katy Klaproth assemble a summary of the quarterly sales data to be included in the board report package. No additional information is needed from any other Marketing and Communications team members for this report.

## Process

Timeline is approximately 6 weeks from initial project list to meeting.

### Step 1: Project List

The list is received from Accounting team and includes top 25-30 projects by contract amount.

The executive team reviews the list and determines the order they'll appear in the report.

### Step 2: Aerials

* Sarah sends out a request via the [Board Report Update board on Monday.com](https://robinsmorton-global.monday.com/boards/4886086839)
* Marketing teams use the Status column to track
  * Tanya Davis handles Florida
  * Sue king handles Huntsville
  * Sarah organizes all in folders in Egnyte

#### Best practices

* Exterior shots
* Show as much of the project as possible
* High-resolution
* Site should appear clean and organized
* Not a rendering unless truly necessary

#### How to submit

* via OpenAsset link
* Directly via email or Teams
* via Egnyte link

### Step 3: Project Updates and Narratives

* Lisa sends requests to OMs via a Jotform. Includes project description, status, estimate/GMP components, potential risks/issues, and other details
* Sarah and copilot format into narratives and Sarah and rachael edit

### Step 4: Design&#x20;

* Content is delivered to graphics team and formatted according to the existing design template for print
* Proofs are reviewed by Sarah, Rachael, then exec team

### Step 5: Final Edits

* Sarah coordinates with OMs and graphics to resolve executive edits and answer questions
* Graphics packages print file&#x20;
* Graphics formats photos and narratives into a corresponding slide deck

## Meeting

* Book is printed and Lisa delivers a copy to each board member
* Powerpoint deck is used during live meeting.
